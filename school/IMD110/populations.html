Did You Know?




Passerines vs. Non-passerines

Generally they're two different categories of birds species, passerines, or birds with 3 toes foward and one toe backwards, and non-passerines which only have 2 toes forward.


Diving Birds

Diving birds are birds which plunge into water to catch fish or other food.


Migrating Birds

Migrating birds are birds that travel to different locations according to the seasons.


Feathers

Feathers are one of the epidermal growths that form the distinctive outer covering, or plumage, on birds and some non-avian theropod dinosaurs. They are considered the most complex integumentary structures found in vertebrates.


Beaks

The beak, bill, or rostrum is an external anatomical structure of birds which is used for eating and for grooming, manipulating objects, killing prey, fighting, probing for food, courtship and feeding young.


Oviparous Animals

Animals that lay eggs to reproduce are called Oviparous Animals.
